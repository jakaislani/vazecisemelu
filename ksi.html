Professional Life of KSI

YouTube:

He started a YouTube channel called ‘JideJunior’ on April 24, 2008. He started his current YouTube account on July 24, 2009. A video on the FIFA video game franchise is included. According to his website, Weepeler and Hjerpseth motivated him to start his own YouTube career. Previously, his main channel focused on association football and FIFA, as well as other games like Grand Theft Auto V. Videos of him playing horror, arcade, and casual games can be found on his other channel.

Following his activities at a Eurogamer event in 2012, he became embroiled in a scandal. He had an appearance in GWRomg’s video “KSI VS FIFA / The Record Slam” in February 2013. In 2013, he was listed in the Guinness World Records Gamer’s Edition for this accomplishment. He signed with Maker Studios’ sub-network in October 2013.

In early 2017, he stopped publishing videos, but in July, he returned with a new video. He quit the Sideman organization, but eventually rejoined it on a formal basis. On August 15, he uploaded a video in which he said he was being deported from the United States because he had received the wrong visa. He published a video in November 2017 in which he addressed the question of whether his alleged battle with the Sidemen was real or phony, noting that “the drama wasn’t fully real, but wasn’t entirely fabricated either.”

Music:

In 2011, he made his music debut as a rapper. He and his fellow YouTuber Randolph published “Heskey Time,” a rap song about footballer Emile Heskey, on Christmas Day in 2011. On his YouTube account, he started a video series called “Football Rap Battles.” He performed in music videos with Droideka, directing and performing in the video for his song Get Hyper. He also collaborated with British rapper Sway, releasing two tracks with him.

KSI released his first solo single, titled “Lamborghini,” on March 23, 2015. He announced his debut EP, ‘Keep Up,’ on October 29, 2015, and it was published on January 8, 2016. KSI released his second EP, titled “Jump Around,” on October 28, 2016. He released his third EP, titled ‘Space,’ on June 30, 2017.

Amateur Boxing Career:

On 3 February 2018, he won his first white-collar amateur boxing contest at Copper Box Arena in London. The YouTube Boxing Championship Belt was presented to him.

KSI’s Award:

He got the NME Award for Vlogger of the Year in 2016.
